Welcome to the first lesson! In this lesson, you will start with Program Mode (P mode) and Auto Focus Mode. These settings are designed to help you get familiar with the basics of photography, allowing you to understand how to take well-exposed photos with your camera. By the end of this lesson, you will have a solid foundation on how to use these modes effectively to capture great images.

Understanding Program Mode (P Mode)

Program Mode, or P Mode, is a semi-automatic mode on your camera. It automatically sets the aperture and shutter speed for you, allowing you to focus on framing your shot and composing your photo. This mode is ideal for beginners as it offers a good balance between control and convenience.

Understanding Auto Focus Mode

Auto Focus Mode helps your camera automatically focus on the subject in the frame. This mode is particularly useful when shooting moving subjects or in situations where you need to quickly capture a moment.

Try it yourself

To operate Program Mode (P Mode) on your camera, turn the mode dial to 'P.' In this mode, the camera automatically sets the aperture and shutter speed, but you can still adjust settings like ISO, white balance, and exposure compensation as needed. For Auto Focus (AF) Mode, switch the camera to 'AF' using the focus mode switch on the lens or camera body. If your lens has a switch labeled 'AF/MF,' set it to 'AF.' Some older or manual lenses might not support autofocus, in which case you would need to manually focus by rotating the focus ring on the lens. In the camera menu, you can also select different focus areas or modes, such as single-point AF, dynamic area AF, or auto-area AF, depending on your subject and shooting situation.
