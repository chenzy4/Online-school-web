Welcome to the second intermediate retouching lesson! In this lesson, you'll dive deeper into specific retouching techniques to enhance your photos using Adobe Photoshop. These techniques include adjusting the subject's position, color correction, creative effects, and removing unwanted elements.

Adjusting the Subject's Position

Aligning the subject correctly within the frame can greatly improve the composition of your photo. Use the crop tool in Photoshop to adjust the subject's position:

  1. Open your photo: Go to File > Open and select your photo.
  2. Select the Crop Tool: Press C on your keyboard or select the Crop Tool from the toolbar.
  3. Adjust the crop box: Drag the edges of the crop box to frame your subject correctly. Use the rule of thirds grid to help with alignment.
  4. Apply the crop: Press Enter to apply the crop.

Color Correction

Accurate colors are essential for a visually appealing photo. Follow these steps for color correction in Photoshop:

  1. Open the Adjustments Panel: Go to Window > Adjustments to open the Adjustments panel.
  2. Adjust White Balance: Select the Curves adjustment layer. Use the white balance dropper tools to set the correct white balance.
  3. Adjust Exposure: Select the Exposure adjustment layer. Use the sliders to correct the exposure.
  4. Adjust Contrast and Saturation: Select the Brightness/Contrast and Hue/Saturation adjustment layers to tweak contrast and color saturation.

Creative Effects

Applying creative effects can give your photos a unique look. Here’s how to apply a vignette effect and a sepia filter in Photoshop:

  1. Vignette Effect:
    • Go to Filter > Lens Correction.
    • In the Lens Correction dialog, go to the Custom tab.
    • Under Vignette, adjust the Amount and Midpoint sliders to create the desired vignette effect.
    • Click OK to apply the effect.
  2. Sepia Filter:
    • Go to Image > Adjustments > Hue/Saturation.
    • Check the Colorize box.
    • Adjust the Hue slider to 30 for a sepia tone.
    • Adjust the Saturation slider to around 25.
    • Click OK to apply the filter.

Removing Unwanted Elements

Removing distractions can focus attention on the subject. Use the Clone Stamp Tool and Healing Brush Tool in Photoshop:

  1. Clone Stamp Tool:
    • Select the Clone Stamp Tool from the toolbar or press S on your keyboard.
    • Alt-click (Option-click on Mac) on the area you want to sample from.
    • Click and drag over the unwanted element to cover it with the sampled area.
  2. Healing Brush Tool:
    • Select the Healing Brush Tool from the toolbar or press J on your keyboard.
    • Alt-click (Option-click on Mac) on the area you want to sample from.
    • Click and drag over the unwanted element. Photoshop will blend the area with the surrounding pixels.
